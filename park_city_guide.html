Epic Outdoor Experiences

Park City offers year-round adventures for every skill level. From the legendary powder skiing that made this town famous to world-class mountain biking trails, discover why Park City is Utah's outdoor adventure capital.

🎿 World-Class Skiing

All Levels

Experience the legendary powder at Park City Mountain Resort and Deer Valley. With over 7,300 acres of skiable terrain, Park City offers everything from gentle beginner slopes to challenging double-black diamonds.

Best Spots: Park City Mountain Resort, Deer Valley Resort

Insider Tip: Visit mid-week for shorter lift lines and better powder conditions

December - April Peak Season

🚵 Mountain Biking

Intermediate to Advanced

Navigate over 400 miles of single-track trails through alpine forests and meadows. The Mid Mountain Trail offers stunning views while technical trails like Wasatch Crest provide thrilling challenges.

Best Spots: Mid Mountain Trail, Wasatch Crest Trail, Park City Mountain Resort

Insider Tip: Start early in summer to avoid afternoon thunderstorms

June - October Epic Views

🥾 Hiking Adventures

All Levels

Explore pristine wilderness with trails ranging from easy nature walks to challenging summit hikes. The Rail Trail offers family-friendly options while trails like Clayton Peak reward hikers with panoramic views.

Best Spots: Rail Trail, Clayton Peak, Guardsman Pass

Insider Tip: Download trail maps offline as cell service can be spotty

May - October Family Friendly

🏔️ Scenic Gondola Rides

All Ages

Enjoy breathtaking 360-degree views without the hike. The Park City Mountain Resort gondola and Deer Valley's Sterling Express provide easy access to alpine dining and stunning vistas.

Best Spots: Park City Mountain Resort, Deer Valley Resort

Insider Tip: Perfect for sunset views and alpine dining experiences

Year-Round Scenic Views

🎣 Fly Fishing

Beginner to Advanced

Cast your line in pristine mountain streams and alpine lakes. The Provo River and local reservoirs offer excellent trout fishing with stunning mountain backdrops.

Best Spots: Provo River, Jordanelle Reservoir, Rockport Reservoir

Insider Tip: Early morning and evening provide the best fishing conditions

May - October Peaceful

🏂 Snowboarding

Intermediate to Advanced

Shred the slopes at two world-renowned resorts. Park City Mountain Resort features extensive terrain parks while Deer Valley offers perfectly groomed runs for carving.

Best Spots: Park City Mountain Resort, Deer Valley Resort

Insider Tip: Deer Valley is ski-only, but Park City Mountain welcomes snowboarders

December - April Powder Days

🛷 Winter Activities

All Ages

Experience winter magic with sledding, snowshoeing, and cross-country skiing. The Rail Trail transforms into a winter wonderland perfect for Nordic skiing and snowshoeing.

Best Spots: Rail Trail, Round Valley, Soldier Hollow

Insider Tip: City Park has the best sledding hill for families

December - March Family Fun

🧗 Rock Climbing

Intermediate to Expert

Scale dramatic rock faces with routes for every skill level. The Wasatch Mountains offer excellent limestone and granite climbing just minutes from downtown.

Best Spots: Little Cottonwood Canyon, Big Cottonwood Canyon

Insider Tip: Spring and fall offer the best climbing weather

April - October Technical

Local Insider Tips

🌤️ Weather Wisdom

Mountain weather changes rapidly. Always check conditions before heading out and pack layers. Summer afternoons often bring thunderstorms, while winter can bring sudden temperature drops.

🎫 Save Money

Purchase lift tickets and activity passes online in advance for significant savings. Many hotels offer package deals that include lift tickets and equipment rentals.

🚌 Transportation

Park City's free transit system connects all major areas. During ski season, riding the bus is often faster than driving and eliminates parking hassles.

🍽️ Dining

Make reservations well in advance, especially during peak seasons. Historic Main Street offers diverse dining options from casual to fine dining.

🏔️ Altitude Awareness

Park City sits at 7,000 feet elevation. Stay hydrated, take it easy your first day, and be aware that alcohol affects you more at altitude.

📱 Apps & Resources

Download trail maps, weather apps, and resort apps before heading out. Cell service can be spotty in remote areas, so offline maps are essential.
